The gap

We measure the machine obsessively. We don't measure what it's doing to us.

The last 18 months poured capital into knowing whether the model is good — evals, observability, agent memory. The human sitting next to it has nothing equivalent. You can audit an agent's every decision. You cannot audit your own.

Where we are · 2026

And the platforms are shipping the surrender amplifier inside the surrender amplifier.

AI is becoming more relational, more persistent, and more accurate about you specifically — each one harder to push back on. Meanwhile the work itself has shifted from process to outcome. The people closest to it described the same week in June 2026:

Every component of the loop gets a maker and a checker — except the human who designs it and signs off. The one moment of judgment left is the one nobody instruments.

The inquiry

What we're actually testing — and what we're not claiming.

The question

Can you see the wanting-to-own fade before output quality ever dips? The candidate signal is the rate you leave a decision unjustified at irreversible doors. Everything else in the field measures surrender after the work already got worse.

The honest caveat

History is blunt: capacities defended only by individual willpower erode — spelling, spatial memory after GPS, attention (2.5 min in 2004 to 47 seconds in 2023). What survived was saved by curriculum, infrastructure, or professional standards. A personal tool helps the person who runs it. It won't save the population.

What Crux is

A v0 instrument — a Claude Code hook that writes a structured digest per session, plus a dry-run detector for the irreversible moments. It exists to find out whether this is measurable at all. It is not the thing that solves it at scale; that's probably infrastructure or curriculum.

Where it goes next

Capture the four-question justification at one-way doors, then re-measure the rubber-stamp rate against the baseline. If the captured fields turn out to be theatre, the plan is to say so and stop. The kill criteria are real.
